SaaS model as revenue multiplier

Intelligent architecture allows any software to scale arbitrarily and fully automatically, incurring costs only when your customers actually use it. The Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model enables serving 10x more customers with the same team size. Furthermore, an operations team was intentionally omitted, creating a sustainable and maintenance-free ZeroOps infrastructure using a cloud-first strategy.

10x more customers
Maintenance-free infrastructure
3x higher team velocity

The Challenge

A customer's software (C# + SQL Server) was operated for 10 years without major modernization. The existing development team reached its capacity limits, and it was not possible to add more customers without losing quality and reliability. Maintenance and care proved to be a challenge and caused high costs. The business model could no longer grow, and the company was at the end of its growth.

Solution: SaaS Model with Google Cloud Platform

The SaaS model describes the characteristic of serving many customers simultaneously with a shared infrastructure and software. For this purpose, the Google Cloud Platform (GCP) was chosen, as it particularly supports the serverless approach. Through the clever selection of managed services (Cloud Run, BigQuery, Dataflow), operational tasks could be skillfully outsourced to GCP, and the development team focused on new development.

  • Automated scaling to zero during inactivity
  • Managed Security & Managed Services (Cloud Run, BigQuery, Dataflow)
  • Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC) with Terraform for customer environments

ZeroOps: Operating Costs = 0

A good infrastructure repairs itself (self-healing) and scales automatically with actual usage (also Cloud-Native Architecture). The team focuses 100% on features instead of servers.
